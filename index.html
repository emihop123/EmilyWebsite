Wild Bees

Bees are very important in our world as they pollinate flowers. Transferring this pollen helps the flowers to continue to grow, which is vital for the planet!

via GIPHY

Bees are currently facing extinction – They're at risk due to climate breakdown and habitat loss. Shifts in the seasons are disrupting flowering times and the availability of food, shelter, and nesting sites. Climate change also brings extremes of drought, heavier rainfall, and flooding. Bees and pollinating insects are struggling to survive.

Wild Bee Types!

There are many different types of wild bees, such as Bumblebees, Honey bees, Carpenter bees, and Leaf-cutter bees!

There are also "domesticated" types of bees that live in
artificial hives. These bees are usually used to produce honey
that will be harvested and sold! The bees that make honey are called
'honeybees.'

Living on all continents except Antarctica are approximately
350 million honey bee colonies, the majority wild.
This equates to one colony for every 23 people. At approximately 10,000 bees per colony
, there are approximately 3.5 trillion individual honey bees
on the planet – or 440 per person.
